about

AP Calculus BC follows the curriculum set forth by the College Board. It is worth the equivalent of two semesters of college credit upon successful completion of the AP examination in May. Students enrolled in this course are required to take this test. Topics covered include library of functions, limits, the derivative, applications of the derivative, definite and indefinite integrals, applications of integration, techniques of integration, infinite series and sequences, etc.

April 4, 2024 | Thoughts on today's quiz.
a) \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n (n+1)3^n}{ 2^{2n+1}} \)
The Ratio Test works best here. Be sure to use the absolute value notation:
\( |\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}| = |\frac{(n+2)3^{n+1}}{2^{2n+3}} * \frac{2^{2n+1}}{(n+1)3^n}| = ... |\frac{n+2}{n+1} \frac{3}{4}|\rightarrow \frac{3}{4} < 1 \)
Since the series converges absolutely, it convergs.

b) \( \sum_{n=1} (-1)^n \frac{\sqrt{n}}{2n+1} \)
Use the alternating series test here. We need to check and show work for two conditions:
First, \( b_n = \frac{\sqrt{n}}{2n+1} = \frac{1/\sqrt{n}}{2+\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}} \rightarrow \frac{0}{2+0} = 0 \)
Second, confirm that \( b_{n+1} \leq b_n\). You can do this algebraically or by taking the derivative of \( f(x) = \frac{\sqrt{x}}{2x+1}\) and confirming that the derivative is negative.
c) \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\cos 3n}{1+(1.2)^n} \)
This is similar to the example from class. We show absolute convergence for the given series by using the comparison test twice:
\( | \frac{\cos 3n}{1+(1.2)^n} | \leq \frac{1}{1+(1.2)^n} < \frac{1}{(1.2)^n} = (5/6)^n \)
The right-most sequence above makes a geometric series that is convergent, so by the Comparison Test, the original series converges absolutely, and therefore it converges.
d) \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-5)^{2n}}{n^2 9^n} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{5^{2n}}{n^2 9^n} \)
Use the ratio test with care to show that the series diverges. Note that: \( a_{n+1} = \frac{5^{2(n+1)}}{(n+1)^2 9^{n+1} }\). The ratio ends up approaching 25/9, which is greater than 1.
April 4, 2024 | Thoughts on problem 15 from today's worksheet.
An odd number can be described as \( 2n-1 \) or \( 2n+1 \) if n is a positive integer.
In the absence of a formula for the product of the odd numbers, we write:
\( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1*3*...*(2n-1)}{n!} \)
Proceed to test using the Ratio Test: \( |\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}| = | \frac{1*3*...*(2n-1)(2n+1)}{(n+1)*n!} \frac{n!}{1*3*...*(2n-1)}| = |\frac{2n+1}{n+1}|\rightarrow 2 > 1 \)
Therefore the series diverges.

January 12, 2024 | Thoughts on today's quiz
The distance function of the boat going south is: \( x(t) = 20t \).
The distance function of the boat going east towards the same dock is \( y(t) = 15-15t \), because at the beginning (t=0) this boat is located 15 km away and its velocity is negative since it is approaching the dock.
Set up the square of the distance as the objective function: \( Q(t) = (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 = 225 (1-t)^2 + 400t^2 \)
The domain is \( 0 \leq t \leq 1 \). Find the derivative: \( Q'(t) = 450(t-1) + 800 t = 1250 t -450 \)
The only critical number is: \( t= \frac{9}{25}\)
Evaluate: \( Q(0)= 225, Q(1) = 400, Q(9/25) = 144 \)
By the Closed Interval Method, the minimum of the square of the distance occurs at the critical number; the correspoding time is 2:21:36 (21 minutes and 36 seconds past 2:00 PM). At this time the distance will be a minimum as well.
January 9, 2024 | Thoughts on today's quiz
In your drawing label the dimensions of the print area as x (width) and y (height).
\( xy = 42 \rightarrow y = 42/x \)
\( Q(x) = (x+3)(y+2) = (x+3)(42/x + 2) = 42 + 2x + 126/x + 6 \) with domain \( x > 0 \)
\( Q'(x) = 2-\frac{126}{x^2} = \frac{2(x^2-63)}{x^2} \)
List three critical numbers and identify only \( x = \sqrt{63} = 3\sqrt{7} \) as part of the domain.
The other dimension of the print area is: \( y = 2\sqrt{7} \)
The dimension of the paper with least area are: \( 3\sqrt{7} + 3 \) by \( 2\sqrt{7} + 2 \)
To justify, use the First Derivative Test for Global Extrema. Note that the numerator of the factored version of the derivative is a quadratic expression. Its graph is a parabola that opens up and changes from negative to positive at the critical number. This implies that the original function has a global minimum at \(x = 3\sqrt{7} \).
The Second Derivative Test for Global Extrema works as well. No matter which test you use, be sure to explain!
